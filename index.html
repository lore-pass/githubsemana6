They create a registry for companies to report prices, stocks and sales

The government established a mandatory monthly information system starting in April. It seeks to monitor prices and supply of inputs. The Chamber of Hardware Stores (Cafara) whose president is Sergio Angiulli from Rosario, showed the sector's concern for the lack of nationally manufactured products

Publicado 18/03/2021

The national government created an information system for stocks, prices and sales of mass consumer products, construction materials, electronics and chemical inputs . The new regime will reach large companies and aims to improve management tools to align inflation with the guideline set by the Minister of Economy. The announcement was made on the same day that Indec reported a 6.1% increase in wholesale prices for February.

The Ministry of Internal Trade will launch in April the Information System for the Implementation of Economic Reactivation Policies (Sipre), in which large companies must report monthly, by system and under sworn declaration, the prices of their products, quantities sold and stocks of final and intermediate goods.

In a first stage, the measure will reach around half a thousand firms in the food and beverage, personal hygiene and cleaning sectors; building materials and furniture; chemical and agrochemical; and electronics and appliances.

The first presentation must be made between April 1 to 10 of this year and, for the only time, the prices, quantities sold and available stock for the months of January, February and March of 2021 will be reported. Then, the monthly procedure will be done reference to the same items but only for the immediately preceding month.

The companies must report the information, which will be reserved and confidential, on a mandatory basis. Failure to do so will be subject to sanctions through the supply law. "This initiative will allow us to have an updated and more precise monitoring to track prices and supplies, detect alerts, production problems," they trusted from the Ministry of Productive Development. It will also allow "to have better information regarding the table of prices and wages."

The Sipre will include companies that annually invoice more than $ 965 million in the case of the construction sector, more than $ 2,602 million in shops and $ 2,540 million in firms dedicated to industrial activity.

The Secretary of Internal Commerce, Paula Español, said that the Sipre will allow to have "a monitoring of prices and the supply throughout the chain", and clarified that it is not an intervention in the market but of "an informative regime" . He explained that its implementation responds to the fact that "the problems of recent months have to do with the lack of some inputs and speculative behavior."

Logically, the president of the Food Products Industries Coordinator (Copal), Daniel Funes de Rioja, criticized the measure. “If this information flow that is being requested is for statistical purposes, it seems inappropriate to me; and if it is for control, there are other means and other ways by which the State can control ”, he said.

Funes de Rioja protested because, at the food agreement table, the businessmen declared that their costs are above prices, which are frozen. But Español responded that "many companies in the food sector had complaints about what was happening with supplies." And in this way, he said, they will be detectable.

Hardware stores demand supplies

Precisely, the Chamber of Hardware and Related Stores of the Argentine Republic (Cafara) yesterday expressed its concern about "the lack of products such as wires, nails, screws and plates" , essential nationally manufactured inputs in industry, construction and homes.

"Although due to the pandemic and the quarantine, the vast majority of manufacturers of hardware products had to alter the rhythm of production due to issues, the high demand of the construction and spare parts sector cannot be neglected," the entity clarified in a statement.

The particular problem of the sector is one of the issues that the national government through the Ministry of Productive Development with the manufacturing companies of different inputs, to respond and regularize the supply of basic inputs to the different industries.

In this sense, the president of Cafara, Sergio Angiulli, said: "We have detected that this problem has been replicated in all businesses in the country, causing strong shortages of supplies in the Argentine domestic market."

According to the entity, the lack of stock leads hardware stores and industry players to have certain problems when it comes to dealing with the needs of their businesses.

Among them, he mentioned the “economic losses” because “the merchant cannot afford the fixed expenses of the trade as he does not have income due to the lack of stock” of the products.
